Technology & Society for Programmers

Imagine that three years from now, you have finished your degree at OsloMet and have just been recruited to one of the largest consultancy firms in Norway. In the first week in your new position, you are told by your supervisor that, in addition to your individual tasks, you will be working with a small team of 5-6 people, who regularly meet to discuss and find good balanced solutions to complex situations and problems within the company or society in general.

Benefits of working in small groups

Group work is advantageous as it enhances creativity. This is because multiple perspectives contribute to different creative solutions. Moreover, the act of discussing itself is a creative process that sets itself apart from independent thinking. Solutions reached collectively in groups tend to be more robust, as they have already undergone a critical process in their development. When working individually, one may be biased in the creation of their own solution. Allocation of responsibilities helps in creating solutions more efficiently and enables responsibility to be assigned to individuals who excel in solving specific problems within their area of expertise."

The importance of disagreeing when solving complex cases

Disagreement is advantageous in complex matters as it involves a critical process while performing the task itself. This leads to any issues with the solution to the complex problem being identified earlier in the process. It is also beneficial because of the diversity of perspectives and skills, which contributes to arriving at the best possible conclusion. By distributing the complex work among different members with varying expertise, a solution can be reached more quickly.

Solutions to "missing" colleagues

If one of the members no longer contributes or communicates with the other members, it would be advantageous to take several steps:

  • The first would be to try as much as possible to get in touch with the individual and find out why they are no longer participating.
  • Attempt to find a solution. If it proves impossible to contact the individual or reach a resolution, it will be necessary to consult with higher-level management at the workplace on how to further address this.
